# Ricardo and Comparative Advantage

Inspired by foundational discussions in international trade, comparative advantage explains why trade raises welfare even if a country is absolutely less efficient in every industry.

- Ricardian two-goods, two-country framework with constant opportunity costs
- Deriving relative prices and specialization ranges
- Policy implications: lowering trade barriers, supporting labor reallocation

Further reading: Krugman, Obstfeld & Melitz, International Economics.
